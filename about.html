Unique Aromas & Flavors


Wines are incredibly diverse, and the world of wine is rich with unique aromas and flavors that can be attributed to various grape varieties, winemaking techniques, and regional influences

  • Petrichor: Some wines, especially those made from certain grape varieties, can have aromas reminiscent of the earth after a fresh rain.
  • Brettanomyces (Brett): While considered a fault in high concentrations, a subtle presence of Brettanomyces.
  • Minerality: The term "minerality" is often used to describe non-fruit and non-herbaceous aromas in wine that are reminiscent of elements like stones, wet rocks, or flint.
  • Eucalyptus: Some red wines, especially those from regions with eucalyptus trees nearby, may exhibit aromas of eucalyptus.
  • Green Bell Pepper (Pyrazines): Some red wines, particularly those made from Cabernet Sauvignon and Merlot, can exhibit aromas of green bell pepper.
